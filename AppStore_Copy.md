## Description (4000 char max)

One Hue is a daily coloring ritual.

Every day, a new artwork appears — the same one for everyone, everywhere. Pick a color from the palette. Tap the numbered regions. Watch the picture come alive in your hands.

That's the whole app.

There's no streak shaming. No login. No "unlock 80 more artworks for $9.99/month." You bought it, it's yours, and it'll quietly hand you a new piece of art tomorrow morning.

WHAT'S IN IT
• 366 hand-curated artworks — one for every day of the year
• Painted in a flat editorial style with bold, limited palettes
• Meditative pacing — fill at your speed, no timer, no score
• A small completion message after each piece, sometimes warm, sometimes a little dry
• Artwork gallery for revisiting anything you've finished
• Daily reminder if you want one (off by default)

WHAT'S NOT IN IT
• No ads
• No accounts or sign-up
• No subscriptions
• No tracking
• No leaderboards
• No "premium" tier — you have all of it

FOR WHOM
For people who want something calm to do with their hands. For commuters. For tea breaks. For waiting rooms. For the moment between work and dinner. For grandmothers and teenagers and everyone in between.

If you've ever felt like apps are designed to take from you, One Hue tries the other thing.

A new artwork lands every day at midnight in your local time. The artwork is the same as everyone else's — somewhere in the world, someone is filling the same swan, the same grove of trees, the same lit window in a winter market. That's the only social thing about it. You're not alone, you're just quiet.

The whole world colors the same page.
